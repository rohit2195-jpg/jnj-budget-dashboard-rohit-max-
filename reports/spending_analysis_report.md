# US Government Spending Analysis: Top & Bottom 5 Recipients

## Executive Summary

This report provides an analysis of US government spending, highlighting the top five and bottom five recipients of award amounts. The findings reveal a significant disparity in spending, with the highest awards directed towards major corporations in the energy, defense, and technology sectors. In contrast, the smallest awards are distributed among a diverse group of small businesses and individuals for highly specific goods and services. The scale of difference between the top and bottom recipients underscores the varied nature and priorities of government procurement.

---

## Additional Insights & Observations

### Stark Contrast in Scale

The most significant observation is the immense gap between the top and bottom of the spending spectrum.
*   **Exxon Mobil Corporation**, the top recipient, was awarded over **$724 million**.
*   **P.M. Brogan, Inc.**, one of the lowest recipients, received **$11,537**.
*   This means the highest award is over **62,700 times larger** than the lowest, illustrating a spending landscape of two different worlds: massive, long-term contracts versus small, specific purchases.

### Notable Trends and Patterns

*   **Strategic Priorities:** The top 5 list clearly signals that national defense, energy security, and technology modernization are dominant federal spending priorities. These areas command billion-dollar contracts awarded to established industry leaders.
*   **Anomalous Entry:** The appearance of the **"PUBLIC EMPLOYEE RETIREMENT SYSTEM, IDAHO"** in the least-spending list is an anomaly. This likely represents a specific, non-standard transaction, such as a reimbursement or a fee, rather than a typical procurement contract for goods or services.
*   **Breadth of Government Needs:** The bottom 5 list, while small in value, demonstrates the sheer breadth of government needs, requiring it to engage with entities ranging from large corporations to individual contractors and small, specialized businesses across the country.
