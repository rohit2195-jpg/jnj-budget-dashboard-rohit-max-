# Analysis of US Government Spending: Top 5 Highest and Lowest Funded Recipients

## Executive Summary

This report provides an analysis of US government spending, highlighting the top five recipients with the highest and lowest total award amounts. The findings reveal a significant disparity in the scale of government awards, with funding for top recipients reaching hundreds of millions of dollars, while the lowest-funded entities receive amounts around eleven thousand dollars.

The highest levels of spending are concentrated in strategic sectors such as energy, defense, and technology, with **EXXON MOBIL CORPORATION** receiving the largest total award. Conversely, the lowest spending is distributed across a diverse range of small businesses, individuals, and specialized service providers.

## Insights and Observations

### Stark Disparity in Funding Scale

The most significant observation is the immense gap between the highest and lowest award amounts. The top recipient, Exxon Mobil, received over **62,700 times** more funding than the lowest recipient, P.M. Brogan, Inc. This highlights the dual nature of government spending, which involves both massive, long-term contracts and numerous small, routine procurements.

### Patterns and Anomalies

*   **Concentration in Strategic Sectors:** The top five recipients are all major players in the energy, defense contracting, and IT/telecommunications industries. This pattern underscores that the bulk of high-value government spending is directed towards national security and infrastructure priorities.
*   **Diversity of Small-Scale Spending:** The lowest-funded recipients are highly varied, including small businesses (P.M. Brogan, Bearing Inspection), a medical corporation, an individual, and even a state retirement system. This reflects the government's need to procure a vast range of goods and services on a much smaller scale.
*   **Notable Anomaly:** The presence of the **Public Employee Retirement System of Idaho** in the lowest spending list is an anomaly. It is not a typical government contractor, suggesting this transaction could be a reimbursement, a specific fee payment, or another unique financial interaction rather than a standard procurement award.
