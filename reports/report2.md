### Summary of US Spending in 2025

The analysis of the US spending data for 2025 reveals a wide range of expenditures, from multi-million dollar contracts to smaller, everyday purchases.

**Key Findings:**

*   **Total Spending:** The total amount awarded in 2025 was **$235,561,320.90** across 440 individual awards.
*   **Average Award:** The average award amount was **$535,366.64**.
*   **Spending Range:** There is a significant gap between the largest and smallest awards, with spending ranging from as low as **$11,558.49** to as high as **$69,116,123.05**.

### Additional Insights & Observations

*   **Nature of Spending:** The highest-value contracts are for specialized services like veteran evaluations, administrative services, and engineering research. This reflects significant government investment in complex, long-term projects. In contrast, the lowest-value awards are for tangible goods like food supplies, representing routine operational expenses.
*   **Vendor Relationships:** It's interesting to note that companies like "US FOODS INC" and "THE MERCHANTS COMPANY, LLC" appear multiple times in the lowest spending list. This suggests that the government makes frequent, smaller purchases from these vendors, likely to supply various federal facilities.
*   **Scale of Government Procurement:** The data clearly illustrates the vast scale and diversity of government procurement. It ranges from securing multi-million dollar services that affect large populations to the basic necessities required to run government operations day-to-day.
