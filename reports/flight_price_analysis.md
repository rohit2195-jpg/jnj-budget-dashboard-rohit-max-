## Executive Summary
This analysis reveals key drivers of airline ticket prices, with flight distance being the most significant factor. The data shows a strong positive correlation of **0.64 correlation coefficient** between distance and price, indicating that longer flights are consistently more expensive. Travel class is also a major determinant, with an average First class ticket costing $3,048.61 USD, nearly triple the price of an Economy ticket. The timing of a ticket purchase has a moderate impact, showing that prices tend to decrease closer to the departure date.

### Average Price by Airline
Saudia has the highest average ticket price at **$2,389.40 USD**. The next most expensive are Turkish Airlines at $2,140.44 USD and Emirates at $2,103.24 USD. Among the airlines analyzed, Etihad offers the lowest average price at $1,486.47 USD.

### Average Price by Class
The most expensive travel class is First class, with an average ticket price of **$3,048.61 USD**. This is followed by Business class at $1,939.81 USD. Economy class is the most affordable option, with an average price of $1,054.49 USD, which is approximately 35% of the cost of a First class ticket.

### Top 15 Most Expensive Routes by Average Price
The route from London to Mumbai is the most expensive, with an average ticket price of **$5,576.32 USD**. The next two most expensive routes are New York to Doha at $5,393.52 USD and Paris to Mumbai at $5,367.82 USD. Of the top 15, the route from Doha to London has the lowest average price at $2,763.31 USD.

### Correlation: Days Before Departure vs. Price
The analysis found a weak negative correlation of **-0.32 correlation coefficient** between the number of days before departure and the ticket price. This suggests that, contrary to common belief, booking further in advance is associated with slightly higher prices for the flights in this dataset.

### Correlation: Distance vs. Price
There is a strong positive correlation of **0.64 correlation coefficient** between flight distance and ticket price. This is the most significant correlation found in the dataset, indicating that the distance of the flight is a primary driver of its cost.

## Additional Insights
The data clearly indicates that luxury travel commands a significant premium. The average price for a First class ticket ($3,048.61 USD) is **189% higher** than the average for an Economy ticket ($1,054.49 USD). This premium is reflected in the high average prices of airlines like Saudia ($2,389.40 USD) and Emirates ($2,103.24 USD), which likely have a larger proportion of premium cabin offerings on the routes analyzed. The strong positive correlation between distance and price (0.64 correlation coefficient) further solidifies that long-haul, premium-cabin travel are the primary drivers of high ticket costs in this dataset.
