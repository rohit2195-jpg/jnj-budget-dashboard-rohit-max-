# Income Analysis Report

## Executive Summary
This analysis reveals that income level is strongly correlated with education, occupation, age, and marital status. A significant majority of individuals, **75.10 percentage**, earn at or below $50K. Education is a primary determinant of higher income, with individuals holding a 'Prof-school' degree having a 74.91 percentage chance of earning over $50K, the highest of any group.

### Overall Income Distribution
The dataset is predominantly composed of individuals in the lower income bracket. **75.10 percentage** of the population earns less than or equal to $50K, while the remaining 24.90 percentage earns more than $50K.

### High Income Percentage by Education Level
Higher education levels are strongly associated with higher income. The top three education levels for earning over $50K are Prof-school (**74.91 percentage**), Doctorate (74.67 percentage), and Masters (56.46 percentage). Conversely, the 'Other' category, which includes lower levels of education, has the lowest high-income rate at just 3.11 percentage.

### Top 15 Occupations by High Income Percentage
Occupation plays a critical role in income potential. 'Exec-managerial' roles have the highest proportion of high earners at **48.53 percentage**, followed by 'Prof-specialty' at 44.87 percentage and 'Protective-serv' at 32.61 percentage. The occupation with the lowest percentage of high earners is 'Priv-house-serv' at a mere 0.71 percentage.

### Average Numeric Features by Income Group
Individuals earning over $50K differ significantly from their lower-earning counterparts across several metrics. They have substantially higher average capital gains (**3938.73 USD** vs. 149.03 USD), are older on average (43.96 years vs. 36.61 years), and work more hours per week (45.71 hours per week vs. 39.35 hours per week). Average capital loss is also higher for the >50K group at 193.80 USD compared to 53.50 USD for the <=50K group.

### High Income Percentage by Marital Status and Sex
Marital status and sex intersect to influence income. Among females, those categorized as 'Married-AF-spouse' have the highest likelihood of earning over $50K at **50.00 percentage**. For males, the 'Married-civ-spouse' category shows the highest propensity for high income at 45.29 percentage. The lowest rate is found among 'Never-married' females, at only 3.79 percentage.

### High Income Percentage by Age Group
Income potential follows a distinct lifecycle pattern. The percentage of high earners peaks in the **50-59** age group at **39.14 percentage**. The lowest earning potential is in the 10-19 age group, where only 0.07 percentage of individuals earn over $50K. After the peak in the 50s, the rate of high earners begins to decline, falling to 27.66 percentage for the 60-69 age group.

## Additional Insights
- **Compounding Factors for High Income:** The data suggests a typical high earner is a male in his 40s or 50s with a professional or doctoral degree, working in a managerial or specialty occupation for over 45 hours per week. For instance, the average age for the >$50K group is **43.96 years**.
- **Gender Disparity:** Across most marital statuses, males have a higher percentage of high-income earners than females. The most significant gap is in the 'Widowed' category, where 27.66 percentage of men earn over $50K compared to just 5.98 percentage of women.
- **Capital Gains as a Differentiator:** The most dramatic numerical difference between income groups is in capital gains. The average capital gain for the >$50K group is over **26 times higher** than for the <=$50K group (3938.73 USD vs. 149.03 USD), indicating that investment income is a major factor for top earners.
